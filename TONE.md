# Succession Holding LLC — Tone & Style Guide

## Voice: The Succession Education Team

Succession publishes real estate education content for independent investors, landowners, and people building long-term wealth through property. Every piece is written by the **Succession Education Team** — an institutional voice. The product is practical, reliable information presented clearly. Our readers are educated adults who want substance, not sales pitches.

## Core Principles

### 1. Educational, Not Sales-Oriented
We explain how things work, not why you should buy our product or sign up for our course. If an article mentions a strategy or approach, we explain the mechanics and tradeoffs — we don't soft-sell it. Readers should feel like they're getting a clear-eyed briefing, not a funnel.

### 2. Factual and Specific
Real estate law, tax treatment, market dynamics — these things involve real numbers and real rules. We are precise. We name states, cite specific statutes, quote actual tax rates. We don't say "many investors" when we mean "investors in Alaska." We don't say "you may be able to deduct expenses" when we can say "Alaska Statute 29.45.080 allows rental property owners to deduct depreciation as an expense against rental income."

### 3. Acknowledge Complexity Honestly
Real estate investing involves tradeoffs, risks, and regional variation. We don't simplify so much that we mislead. When a tax strategy depends on your specific situation, we say so and recommend consulting a professional. When market conditions vary by city or neighborhood, we say so rather than making sweeping claims.

### 4. No Political Framing
We do not frame real estate or economic analysis through political lenses. We do not discuss political parties, ideological agendas, or social movements in relation to real estate markets or investment outcomes. We do not use politically valanced language in our analysis of housing markets, zoning policy, or investment strategy.

### 5. No Climate or Social Justice Content
Succession covers real estate education: property law, LLC structure, rental income management, Alaska market dynamics, tax strategy, investment analysis. We do not cover climate change, ESG, social justice frameworks, or identity-based analysis of housing markets. We do not editorialize about housing policy through political frames. This is a scope statement, not a political one.

### 6. Professional, Not Casual
We write for educated adults, like Time Magazine correspondents covering a technical topic. Sentences are complete and properly constructed. Terms are defined when first used. We don't use ALL CAPS, excessive exclamation points, or hyperbolic language. No "this amazing opportunity" or "revolutionary strategy." Cool, clear, professional.

## Tone Examples

### What We Sound Like (Correct)
> "Alaska Statute 29.45.080 permits rental property owners to claim depreciation as an income tax deduction over a 27.5-year recovery period for residential property. The deduction reduces taxable rental income but does not represent an out-of-pocket expense — it is a non-cash allocation that reduces the property's cost basis over time. Investors should consult a licensed tax advisor to determine how depreciation interacts with their overall tax situation."

> "The Anchorage residential rental market has shown vacancy rates of approximately 3.2% as of Q1 2026, below the national average of 5.8%. This tighter supply has supported rent growth of approximately 4.1% year-over-year, though the rate varies significantly by neighborhood and property type."

### What We Don't Sound Like (Incorrect)
> "The woke ESG movement is destroying property values in blue cities across America, and smart investors are fleeing to Alaska where common sense still reigns. This is an amazing opportunity that won't last long!"

> "Systemic inequality in housing markets means that Black and Latino families face barriers to wealth-building through real estate that white families don't encounter. Until we address structural racism in housing policy, the dream of property ownership will remain out of reach for millions."

## Formatting Standards

- **Title case** for headlines and section headers
- **No star ratings** — qualitative language only
- **Named jurisdictions** — always specify the state or locality being discussed
- **Legal citations** — statute names and numbers when available
- **Disclaimer placement** — every article with financial or tax content includes a standard disclaimer noting the educational nature of the content and recommending professional consultation
