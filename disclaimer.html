Real Estate Investing Involves Risk

Investing in real estate — whether directly in properties or through funds, REITs, or other structured vehicles — carries material risks that investors should understand before committing capital. These risks include but are not limited to:

  • Loss of principal invested
  • Illiquidity — real estate cannot be converted to cash as quickly as publicly traded securities
  • Market risk — property values can decline based on local economic conditions, oversupply, or demographic shifts
  • Credit risk — tenant default or vacancy can reduce or eliminate cash flow
  • Interest rate risk — rising rates can increase financing costs and reduce property values
  • Regulatory risk — changes in landlord-tenant law, zoning, rent control, or tax policy can affect returns
  • Leverage risk — financing amplifies both gains and losses

Consult a Qualified Professional

Before making any investment decision, you are strongly encouraged to:

  • Consult a licensed real estate professional who can evaluate specific properties and markets
  • Consult a financial advisor or certified public accountant (CPA) regarding your overall financial plan and tax situation
